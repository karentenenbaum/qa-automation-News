Dry weekend ahead for Southern California after more rain, heavy snow in mountains

By EMILY RASMUSSEN | erasmussen@scng.com | | Long Beach Press-Telegram
PUBLISHED: March 11, 2021 at 2:52 pm | UPDATED: March 11, 2021 at 3:22 pm

This week's storm in Southern California was expected to bring more snow to the mountains and possibly some rain to the area, but forecasters said Thursday it will dry out over the weekend.

So far, the cold winter storm has brought about an inch to an inch and a half of rain in Orange County and the Inland Empire, and half an inch to an inch in Los Angeles County. Mountainous areas received about a foot to a foot and a half of snow starting Thursday afternoon, with up to another 8 inches of snow through Friday.

Another quarter-inch to one-third of an inch of rain could fall in Orange County and in the Inland Empire by the end of Friday. About a quarter-inch to a half-inch could fall in Los Angeles County.

“This storm should be weakening going into (Friday),” said Ryan Kittell, a National Weather Service meteorologist. “But we might see a few lingering showers in the mountains.

“But we’re expecting a dry weekend.”

This year’s rainy season, which starts on Oct. 1, has brought about 5.5 inches of rain to downtown Los Angeles. The average normal for this time of year is 12 inches, Kittell said.

For John Wayne Airport in Orange County, there have been about 4 inches of rain so far, 7 inches below normal, said James Brotherton, another meteorologist.

There is still a chance for more showers in March and April, but Kittell said it’s “more than likely we’ll be well below normal this year.”

Although rain and snow are expected to finish by the weekend, cold temperatures are expected to stick around for a few days.

“It’s going to be chilly like it’s been,” Brotherton. “Starting Saturday, it’s going to be dry all through next week. Temperatures will not get warm but close to normal, so back into the 60s and 70s.”

The high temperature forecasted for Long Beach was 58 degrees on Friday and 61 on Saturday. Santa Ana’s high was 60 degrees on Friday and 64 on Saturday.

Riverside’s forecasted high for Friday was 57 and for Saturday was 63 degrees. In the San Bernardino Mountains, Big Bear’s forecasted high was 32 degrees on Friday and 39 on Saturday.

The cold temperatures in the mountains will keep snow on the ground and make road conditions icy, Brotherton said.

“Be careful traveling over the mountains in the next couple of days,” he said. “People should bring emergency supplies and expect to have travel delays or possibly be stranded.”
